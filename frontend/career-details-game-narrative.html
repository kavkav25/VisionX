Game Narrative Design

Game narrative design in India is an exciting and evolving field that blends creativity with technology. As the gaming industry grows and diversifies, the demand for talented narrative designers continues to rise. Those passionate about storytelling and interactive experiences have the opportunity to shape the future of gaming through compelling narratives that captivate players and enhance their gaming experience.

Career Details

• Core Skills & Responsibilities: Narrative designers develop plotlines, character arcs, and dialogue scripts. They collaborate closely with game designers, artists, and programmers to weave the narrative seamlessly into gameplay. Responsibilities include writing compelling narratives, creating lore, designing branching storylines, and ensuring consistency in the game's world-building.
• Growth Potential: With the booming gaming industry in India and globally, the demand for skilled narrative designers is increasing. As games become more story-driven and complex, narrative design plays a crucial role in attracting players and enhancing their experience.

Educational Requirements

o After 10th: Choose a stream in 11th and 12th that emphasizes Arts, Literature, or Computer Science to build a strong foundation.
o After 12th: Pursue a Bachelor's degree in Game Design, Creative Writing, English Literature, or Multimedia Arts.
o Postgraduate: A Master's degree in Game Design, Interactive Storytelling, or a related field can provide advanced skills and deeper industry insights.

Relevant Skills

o Strong writing and storytelling abilities for creating engaging narratives and dialogues.
o Understanding of game mechanics and how narrative integrates with gameplay.
o Creativity and imagination to develop unique characters and storylines.
o Knowledge of player psychology and how narrative influences player experience.
o Collaboration skills to work effectively with diverse teams, including designers, artists, and programmers.
o Familiarity with game development tools and software (e.g., Unity, Unreal Engine).

Entry exams

To gain admission into undergraduate degree programs in game narrative design in India, most institutions primarily consider students' performance in their 12th-grade examinations. However, several universities and colleges also conduct entrance exams or assessments for admission. Some notable entrance assessments include:

o Design Aptitude Test (DAT): Conducted by the National Institute of Design (NID) and other design colleges, this exam evaluates creativity and design thinking, which are crucial for game narrative design.
o Symbiosis Entrance Test (SET): For programs offered by Symbiosis Institute of Design, which may include game design and narrative-focused courses.
o B.Des Entrance Exam: Various institutions may have their own design entrance exams specifically tailored for Bachelor of Design programs, which include narrative design components.
o Portfolio Review: Some colleges may require a portfolio showcasing creative projects, writing samples, or previous work related to narrative design or game development.

These entrance exams and assessments typically evaluate students' creative thinking, storytelling abilities, and understanding of game mechanics and design principles.

Top Institutions for Game Narrative Design in India

o Drew University, New Jersey (with partnerships for online courses) – Offers a degree in Game Design focusing on narrative aspects.

o Srishti Institute of Art, Design and Technology, Bangalore – Offers a B.Des. in Game Design and focuses on interactive storytelling.

Common Roles

o Narrative Designer: Creates the storyline, characters, and dialogue for video games, ensuring the narrative aligns with gameplay.
o Game Writer: Focuses on writing scripts, character dialogue, and in-game text, collaborating with designers and developers.
o Story Consultant: Provides expertise on narrative structure, character development, and storytelling techniques for game projects.
o World Builder: Develops the game world, including its history, culture, and geography, ensuring consistency with the narrative.
o Character Designer: Works closely with narrative designers to create compelling characters and backstories that enhance gameplay.
o Content Designer: Integrates narrative elements into the game’s user experience, ensuring the story flows seamlessly with gameplay mechanics.

Suitable for Specially Abled Individuals

• Visually Impaired: Roles focused on writing, such as scriptwriting or narrative development, may be adapted for visually impaired individuals, especially with the use of assistive technologies.
• Hearing Impaired: Narrative design roles can be suitable, as they often involve writing and conceptual work that doesn't require auditory skills.
• Mobility Impaired: Many aspects of narrative design can be done remotely or in accessible workspaces, focusing on writing and collaborative projects.
