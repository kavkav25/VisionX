Counsellor

Counselling in India is a growing and rewarding field, offering numerous opportunities to specialize in areas such as mental health, career counselling, family therapy, and corporate counselling. As awareness of mental health and well-being increases, so does the demand for trained counsellors. Whether working in schools, hospitals, corporate offices, or private practice, counsellors play a vital role in supporting individuals through life’s challenges. While the path to becoming a counsellor requires commitment to higher education and practical experience, the personal and financial rewards of helping others achieve emotional well-being are significant.

Career Details

• Core Skills & Responsibilities: Counsellors are trained to assess clients' issues, offer coping strategies, and create a safe, confidential environment for discussing personal matters. The role requires active listening, communication, empathy, and ethical decision-making.
• Growth Potential: In India, mental health awareness has been growing, leading to increased demand for counsellors in educational institutions, corporations, and healthcare settings. Counsellors can specialize in various fields, including mental health, career counselling, family therapy, and school guidance.

Educational Requirements

o After 10th: Choose any stream (Arts, Science, or Commerce). Opting for Psychology, Sociology, or related subjects can provide an early understanding of human behavior.
o After 12th: Enroll in a Bachelor's degree in Psychology, Social Work, or a related field. Follow this with a Master's in Psychology, Counselling Psychology, or Clinical Psychology.

Relevant Skills

o Strong communication and interpersonal skills.
o Active listening and empathy.
o Ability to assess emotional and psychological issues.
o Ethical decision-making and confidentiality.
o Knowledge of mental health issues, psychological assessments, and therapeutic interventions.

Entry exams

To get into undergraduate degree programs in psychology or counseling in India, most institutions admit students based on their 12th-grade marks. However, some universities and colleges conduct entrance exams for admission. A few notable ones include:
o CUET (Common University Entrance Test): Conducted for admissions to central universities like Delhi University, Banaras Hindu University, etc.
o Christ University Entrance Test: For admission to psychology and counseling programs at Christ University, Bangalore.
o Symbiosis Entrance Test (SET): For admission to Symbiosis School for Liberal Arts, which offers psychology programs.
o TISS-BAT: For the BA program in Social Sciences at Tata Institute of Social Sciences.

These tests generally assess aptitude, reasoning, and basic understanding of psychology.

Top Institutions for Counselling in India

o University of Delhi (DU) – M.A. in Psychology or Applied Psychology.

o Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi – M.A. in Counselling Psychology.

Common Roles

o School Counsellor: Provides guidance to students on academic, social, and emotional issues.
o Career Counsellor: Helps individuals make informed career choices based on aptitude, interests, and opportunities.
o Mental Health Counsellor: Offers therapy for individuals dealing with emotional, psychological, or behavioral issues.
o Marriage and Family Counsellor: Provides counselling for families or couples facing relationship challenges.
o Rehabilitation Counsellor: Works with individuals recovering from addiction, trauma, or serious illnesses.
o Corporate Counsellor: Provides mental health services and stress management strategies for employees in corporate settings.
o Crisis Intervention Counsellor: Assists individuals or communities dealing with trauma, such as natural disasters or personal tragedies.
o Clinical Psychologist: Specializes in diagnosing and treating mental disorders through therapy and counselling (requires advanced training).

Suitable for Specially Abled Individuals

• Visually Impaired: Individuals with visual impairments can thrive in this profession, especially in talk-based therapy or tele-counselling, which doesn’t require visual inputs.
• Hearing Impaired: With accommodations like sign language interpreters or the use of written communication tools, hearing-impaired individuals can excel in counselling, especially in online or written counselling roles.
• Mobility Impaired: Counsellors with mobility challenges can easily work in office settings, clinics, or even from home via online platforms. Tele-counselling and remote consultation roles are especially accessible.
